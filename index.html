Subhojit Roy

Postdoctoral Fellow
High Energy Physics Division, Argonne National Laboratory, USA

I am a theoretical particle physicist working on beyond the Standard Model (BSM) phenomenology. My research connects collider physics, dark matter, electroweak phase transitions, gravitational waves, and neutrino phenomenology to uncover the fundamental symmetries that shaped the early Universe. I obtained my Ph.D. from Harish-Chandra Research Institute (under HBNI) in 2023, advised by Prof. AseshKrishna Datta. At Argonne, I work with Prof. Carlos E.M. Wagner and Dr. Katrin Heitmann.

Research Interests

Colliders

BSM & Collider Phenomenology

Searches for new physics at HL-LHC, Muon Collider, and EIC using cut-based and ML analyses (BDTs, likelihood methods). Probing unprobed regions of SUSY, extended Higgs, and Higgs-portal scenarios. Testing proton structure and lepton PDFs.

Early Universe

Electroweak Baryogenesis & Phase Transitions

Advances in thermal resummation schemes for finite-temperature field theory. Improved bubble-wall dynamics in first-order phase transitions. Baryon asymmetry generation via spontaneous CP violation. GW signatures testable at PTAs and LISA.

Dark Sector

Dark Matter Phenomenology

Direct and astrophysical indirect detection probes. Higgs-portal inelastic dark matter with coannihilation. Relic density dilution from first-order transitions. Galactic Center gamma-ray excess via Fermi-LAT and CTAO.

Cosmology

Dynamical Dark Energy & LSS

Interacting dark matterâ€“dark energy frameworks motivated by DESI BAO results. Mass-varying dark matter and phantom-like equation of state. EFT of large-scale structure and primordial non-Gaussianity with SPHEREx.

Neutrinos

Neutrino Physics & Anomalies

Neutrino mass-generation via type-I seesaw with radiatively stable cancellations. Heavy neutral lepton searches at muon colliders. Unified explanation of LSND, MiniBooNE, and ATOMKI anomalies via 17 MeV pseudoscalar.

GW Cosmology

Gravitational Waves & Cosmic Defects

Stochastic GW backgrounds from cosmic strings, domain walls, and supercooled transitions. Primordial black hole formation from supercooled phase transitions. Combined PTA spectral fits using PTArcade.
