I am a theoretical particle physicist working on beyond the Standard Model (BSM) phenomenology. My research connects collider physics, dark matter, electroweak phase transitions, gravitational waves, neutrino phenomenology, and dynamical dark energy to uncover the fundamental symmetries that shaped the early Universe. I obtained my Ph.D. from Harish-Chandra Research Institute (under HBNI) in 2023, advised by Prof. AseshKrishna Datta. I am currently a Postdoctoral Fellow at Argonne, working with Prof. Carlos E.M. Higgs Portal Dark Matter & Baryogenesis

Gamma-Rays and Gravitational Waves from Inelastic Higgs Portal Dark Matter

D. Hooper, G. Krnjaic, D. Rocha, S. Roy

arXiv:2507.22975 [hep-ph]

We propose a Higgs portal inelastic scalar dark matter model in which a complex singlet splits into quasi-degenerate states, suppressing direct detection and setting the relic abundance via coannihilation while triggering a first-order electroweak phase transition. The same dynamics account for the Galactic-Center gamma-ray excess and yield line-to-continuum gamma-ray spectra testable by Fermi-LAT and CTAO.

Dark Matter and Electroweak Baryogenesis with Spontaneous CP Violation in the Early Universe

S. Roy

arXiv:2509.19982 [hep-ph]

I extended the Higgs-portal model with a spontaneously CP-violating dimension-6 operator that simultaneously accommodates electroweak baryogenesis and dark matter while explaining the Galactic Center gamma-ray excess. CP violation occurs only during the electroweak phase transition and switches off afterward, allowing the model to evade stringent electric dipole moment (EDM) constraints.

Electroweak Phase Transitions & Thermal Resummation

Self Consistent Thermal Resummation: A Case Study of the Phase Transition in 2HDM

P. Bittar, S. Roy, C. E. M. Wagner

JHEP 12 (2025) 021 ‚Ä¢ arXiv:2504.02024

For the first time, we implemented the tadpole resummation (partial dressing) scheme in a realistic BSM model ‚Äî the Two-Higgs-Doublet Model (2HDM). We demonstrated how conventional Arnold-Espinosa and Parwani schemes can produce spurious results such as symmetry non-restoration at very high temperatures. The strength and dynamics of the phase transition, including bubble nucleation, change significantly depending on the resummation scheme. We estimated the resulting theoretical uncertainty in the predicted gravitational-wave signal.

LOOKING AHEAD

Future Research Directions

COSMOLOGY

Dynamical Dark Energy & Mass-Varying Dark Matter

Motivated by DESI BAO results hinting at w(a) ‚â† ‚àí1, I am studying interacting dark-sector models where dark matter and dark energy evolve jointly through a dynamical scalar field. Scenarios with field-dependent dark matter mass lead to effective phantom-like behavior (w < ‚àí1), connecting DESI's hints to large-scale structure observables.

GW COSMOLOGY

Nano-Hertz Gravitational Waves & Primordial Black Holes

Exploring dark-sector models that simultaneously reproduce the stochastic GW signals observed by NANOGrav (from phase transitions around the 100 MeV scale) and create favorable conditions for primordial black hole (PBH) formation. Combined fits of cosmic strings, domain walls, and first-order transitions using PTArcade.

NEUTRINOS

Vanishing Neutrino Masses & Accidental Lepton Number Conservation

Exploring radiatively stable cancellations in the dimension-5 Weinberg operator arising from specific alignments of Dirac Yukawa and Majorana mass matrices in the type-I seesaw. This mechanism implies an approximate lepton-number symmetry leading to distinctive collider signatures.

BARYOGENESIS

Electroweak Baryogenesis & Dynamical CP Violation

Studying spontaneous CP-violation scenarios at finite temperature with refined bubble-wall dynamics and transport effects for successful baryon asymmetry generation, along with corresponding collider and cosmological signatures.

COLLIDERS & ML

Quantum Information & Machine Learning for Collider Physics

Expanding toward quantum-information and ML-based approaches for collider phenomenology ‚Äî leveraging entanglement, correlation structure, and data-driven inference to characterize complex events at HL-LHC, muon collider, and FCC.

DARK MATTER

Multi-Messenger Dark Matter Searches

Advancing multi-wavelength and multi-messenger DM searches combining gamma- and X-ray observations with laboratory probes of both heavy and light dark sectors, including GW signatures and emerging sensor-based techniques for axion-like and ultralight candidates.
